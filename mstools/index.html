Study Tips

Spaced Repetition — Review material at increasing intervals (today → 3 days → 1 week) to lock it into long-term memory.
Active Recall — Instead of re-reading notes, close the book and write down everything you remember. It's more effective than passive review.
Pomodoro Technique — Study for 25 minutes, then take a 5-minute break. After four rounds, take a longer 20–30 minute break.
Teach It Back — Explain a concept out loud as if teaching someone else. Gaps in your understanding become obvious immediately.
Eliminate Distractions — Put your phone in another room while studying. Even silent phones on a desk reduce working memory by up to 20%.
Sleep Is Study Time — Memory consolidation happens during sleep. Pulling an all-nighter before a test usually hurts more than it helps.
